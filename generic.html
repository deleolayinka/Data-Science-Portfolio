About Me

I am a passionate, results-driven Data Scientist with a strong foundation in statistical analysis, machine learning, and data-driven decision-making. Over the years, I have honed my ability to transform raw data into actionable insights that drive business growth and operational efficiency. I firmly believe in the power of teamwork, collaboration, and the continuous pursuit of knowledge to create meaningful impact. My work ethos is built on professionalism, trust, and a genuine love for solving complex problems through data.

I hold three degrees in Statistics, including a master’s in Data Analytics from Aston University, UK (2023), a master’s degree (Distinction, 2018), and a bachelor’s degree (First Class Honours, 2014) from the University of Ilorin, Nigeria. My academic background, coupled with over seven years of professional experience in data science, has enabled me to excel in both technical and business environments.

Currently, I work as a Data Scientist at Canadian Blood Services, where I utilize machine learning and predictive analytics to optimize donor engagement and enhance operational processes. My previous experience as a Data Scientist at the Nigeria Inter-Bank Settlement Systems (NIBSS) allowed me to lead high-impact projects, such as developing predictive models to forecast product demand and client attrition. My work directly contributed to a 52% reduction in resource costs and improved service uptime.

In recent years, I have expanded my expertise in data visualization and storytelling, creating dynamic dashboards using Power BI that guide executive decision-making. I am passionate about helping organizations leverage data for strategic insights, and I am equally committed to mentoring and supporting my peers in achieving their professional goals.

Looking ahead, my goal is to continue evolving as a data scientist by exploring advanced techniques in machine learning and deepening my skills in presenting data insights. I am always eager to collaborate with like-minded professionals and thrive in environments where innovation and continuous learning are prioritized.
